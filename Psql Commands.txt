1      Connect to a Database: “psql -d db_name -U user_name”.
2	Check Postgres Version: “SELECT VERSION();”.
3	List All Databases: “\l”.
4	Access or Switch a Database: “\c db_name”.
5	List All Tables: “\dt”.
6	Describe All Tables: “\d”.
7	Describe a Specific Table: “\d tab_name”.
8	List All Schemas: “\dn”.
9	List All Views: “\dv”.
10	List All Functions: “\df”.
11	List All Users: “\du”.
12	Show Commands History: “\s”
13	Save Query’s Results to a Specific File: “\o file_name”.
14	Run psql Commands/queries From a Particular File: “\i file_name”.
15	Execute Previous Command: “\g”.
16	Show Query Execution Time: “\timing”.
17	Get Output in HTML Format: “\H”.
18	Align Columns Output: “\a”.
19	Get Help: “\h”.
20	Get All psql Commands: “\?”.
21	Clear Screen: “\! cls”.
22	Quit psql: “\q”.
